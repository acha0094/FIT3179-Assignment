Covid-19 Malaysia Data Analysis

(as of 19 September 2022)

Malaysia Total COVID-19 Cases Per Year

Malaysia was hit the hardest in the year 2021 due to the Covid-19 virus with an alarmingly high number of positive cases. From the interactive map, we can observe how each state fared against other states in dealing with the pandemic. An important thing to note is how the state of Perlis maintained a very low number of positive cases whilst the other states suffered significantly with massive amount of outbreaks. In addition, Sarawak did well in preventing the virus from spreading as can be seen in the year 2022 whereby their number of positive cases dropped significantly compared to the previous year which other states fail to accomplish.

Malaysia COVID-19 Daily New Cases

Based on the chart above, we can see the trend of infections for each state in Malaysia. Notice how there are four main waves of large infections. First wave which began in Sabah occurred between October 2020 to April 2021, which signaled the beginning of the worst to come. The next wave which is the second largest number of infections began only a few weeks after the first wave ended all the way until November 2021. Then, Malaysia encountered its largest wave of infection between February 2022 to May 2022 which fortunately, did not result in significant damages as a majority of the people have already been administered with vaccination shots. Interestingly, the final wave is unique as it had only affected people within the Klang Valley, namely Selangor and Kuala Lumpur.

Malaysia COVID-19 Daily Vaccination Shots

Based on the area chart above, we can see that around 70 million vaccination doses have been administered to the citizens of Malaysia. Making a conservative estimate of RM38.50 per dose, the government has already spent more than RM2.7 billion to purchase various Covid-19 vaccination shots. Keep in mind that this does not include the cost of 2nd booster dose and additional vaccination doses which weren't administered. Source: https://codeblue.galencentre.org/2021/12/03/covid-19-vaccines-for-malaysia-cheaper-than-belgium-pac-report/
